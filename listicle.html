Stanford: 4 on-campus beverages you should be drinking

Victoria Kirst
Last updated Apr 7, 2017

1. Italian soda at Bytes

350 Serra Mall, Stanford, CA

When's the last time you've had an Italian soda? It had been a while for me, but I wanted to join my friend for a non-caffeinated beverage at Bytes and I became reacquainted with the combination of fizzy water and artificial fruit syrup. Italian sodas are not so sweet as a normal soda and not so boring as a flavored carbonated water. The Italian sodas at Bytes also have cream in them (or maybe they do if you ask, I haven't tried), which makes it a nice coffee shop alternative for the times when you're feeling a refreshing drink that doesn't have caffeine or milk.

Recommendation: Raspberry Italian soda

2. Bubble tea at Pop Tea Bar

459 Cambridge Ave, Palo Alto, CA

When your boba craving strikes, head over to Pop Tea Bar, a cute little bubble tea shop on Cambridge Avenue. The menu is your standard bubble tea fare with nothing too adventurous on the menu — no bubble tea floats, for example — but the classics is fresh, the options is fast, and the tea is smooth and refreshing. I like that they have a good selection of non-caffeinated fruit teas along with the typical milk teas, and I usually go for a passionfruit green tea or a mango green tea.

Recommendation: Mango green tea with boba, 25% sugar

3. Latte at CoHo

459 Lagunita Dr, Stanford, CA

I am a latte drinker, which has a particularly amusing quality about it with coffee from Stanford Main Library (colloquially called the "CoHo"). The CoHo latte is a good size — not too small, not too large — and has a good balance of coffee flavor and milk. It's supposed to have latte art (when I go there) but I've never seen it. The CoHo Main Library is colloquially called the "CoHo" and what is.

Recommendation: Medium latte

4. Agua Fresca at Nexus Cafe

Nexus Cafe

Nexus Cafe has a lot of great things going for it: a cafeteria layout, including deep Pho bowls and a lot of fresh, clean and fruity beverage before you head back to the library or off to class, and the agua fresca is a drink on the patio with a cup of agua fresca, and maybe a burrito.

Recommendation: Watermelon agua fresca
